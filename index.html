📂 Real-World Cybersecurity Cases

This page highlights real cybersecurity incidents with actionable takeaways. These summaries include causes, impacts, and lessons learned to support organizational awareness and resilience.

🧊 Snowflake Inc. Breach (2024)

What Happened: Hackers exploited unencrypted credentials stored on an employee’s computer and in a project management tool.

Cause: Weak credential management and lack of encryption protocols.

Damage: Breach of sensitive client data affecting AT&T, Ticketmaster, and more. Major operational and reputational fallout.

Estimated Cost: Not publicly disclosed, but affected data covered hundreds of millions of users.

Resolution: Snowflake implemented tighter credential handling and multi-layered access auditing post-breach.

Source: BleepingComputer

🏥 Change Healthcare Ransomware Attack (2024)

What Happened: A ransomware group (BlackCat/ALPHV) exploited compromised credentials of a low-level support employee with no MFA.

Cause: Lack of multi-factor authentication and internal privilege controls.

Damage: Breach of 100 million records, disrupting one-third of U.S. healthcare transactions.

Estimated Cost: $2.457 billion in damages, with $1.5 billion in direct response costs.

Resolution: Required full infrastructure rebuild, improved employee access policies, and mandatory MFA across systems.

Source: CNN

📦 Maersk Ransomware Incident (2017)

What Happened: The global shipping giant was hit by the NotPetya ransomware, halting operations across 600 ports and terminals.

Cause: Malware infiltrated via infected accounting software used in Ukraine.

Damage: Worldwide operational shutdown; wiped 49,000 laptops and 1,000 apps.

Estimated Cost: $300 million in losses.

Resolution: Rebuilt infrastructure from backups, including a clean domain controller found in a Ghana office.

Source: Wired

🔐 Capital One Data Breach (2019)

What Happened: A former AWS employee exploited a misconfigured firewall to access 100M customer records stored on Amazon S3.

Cause: Server misconfiguration allowing unauthorized access.

Damage: Breach of names, addresses, credit scores, and bank details.

Estimated Cost: Over $300 million in fines, remediation, and legal costs.

Resolution: Capital One enhanced its configuration scanning, encryption enforcement, and external audit process.

Source: New York Times

🎮 Rockstar Games Leak (2022)

What Happened: A hacker obtained early development footage of Grand Theft Auto VI and leaked it online.

Cause: Compromised Slack credentials and poor endpoint security.

Damage: Major intellectual property exposure, loss of control over narrative and market perception.

Estimated Cost: Not officially disclosed, but estimated reputational losses were substantial.

Resolution: Strengthened internal access controls and upgraded development environment security.

Source: BBC News
